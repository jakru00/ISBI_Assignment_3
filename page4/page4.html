Biking in Stockholm

Bike Trips in and near Stockholm

Swedens capital is a beautiful city. If you ever come there, you should consider doing some biking tours since it's one of the most enjoyable ways to discover it's centre and the nature. There are many different trails - some to do sightseeing, short and beginner friendly ones, longer trails for a whole day trip, mountainbike trails and much more.
This page will give you an overview about some recommended bike tours for different purposes. Just check them out and take whatever suits your visit in Stockholm best!

Bike trips to discover the city centre

  • One of the best ways to discover the city centre is doing a bike tour in Norrmalm. The trip is about 30km and suitable for all skill levels. You will pass some of Stockholms loveliest landmarks such as Stockholms Stadshus, the opera, the Vasa Museum, Stockholms slott or Gamla Stan. This tour is very beginner friendly and invites everyone to just stop at all the lovely spots, take some pictures or enjoy one of the famous cinnamon buns in Gamla stan.
  • If you're more interested in some calm places you should make a trip to Hagaparken. It's in the north of Stockholm and has beautiful paths. You could either just cycle through the park to have a short trip or you could add a tour around the lake nearby.
  • A more advanced trip is the Drottningholms Tour. It's about 80km long, so more suitable for a whole day trip.

Bike trips to discover the nature

  • One famous route near Stockholm is the Viking Route. It's a trip around Lake Erken which is situated north east of Stockholm. The trip route is about 35km long and is suitable for all skill levels. If you want to, you could even take a detour to the villages Malsta or Lohärad.
  • Mountain bike lovers should go on a bike trip in the south of Stockholm from Rönninge to Lida and back. It's a more advanced tour with some up and downs and is about 57km long. The trip is full of nature with paths through forests and some beautiful lake views.
